Sustainability and Maintenance Plan

Status. Final draft for Data360 Global Challenge phase 2 (2026-05-29).

Framing

"The Challenge is the door to a partner ecosystem, not a terminal prize. The project connects civic tech, media and startups at scale."
— Mariano Blejman (Media Party), 2026-05-21 office hour.

Data360 News Agent is built to be a public good for public-interest media in the Global South, and a B2B software product for data-publishing institutions. It is the working proof that the World Bank's knowledge bank can be made AI-ready and discoverable for newsrooms — preserving data quality, authoritative sourcing and trustworthiness — without the publisher having to build a single line of bespoke editorial software.

The canonical one-liner of the project:

An AI-powered news agency that detects newsworthy facts across Data360's 12,000 indicators and delivers them, verified and locally framed, to LAC newsrooms.

1. The product behind the demo

The prototype is not a single-deployment showcase. It is the reference instance of a pipeline designed for any institution that publishes a large catalogue of indicators and wants its data to translate into editorial reach — multilateral banks, the IMF, regional commissions like CEPAL, national statistical offices, open-data foundations.

The thesis is operational, not aspirational: data portals receive traffic but not coverage. A modern publisher curates thousands of indicators and has no internal newsroom to turn them into stories. Our pipeline performs that conversion — verifiable, bilingual, daily — without the institution having to build editorial capacity in-house, and without compromising newsroom independence on the receiving end.

Two service modes

We expect first deployments to fall into one of two configurations:

Mode What Abrimos delivers Fit
Operated Abrimos installs, runs and maintains the pipeline on the client's catalogue; weekly editorial output flows to the client's chosen distribution channels Institutions without internal data or dev capacity, or wanting a turnkey publication channel
Enabled We deploy the stack, train the client's data and communications team to operate it, and provide quarterly support Institutions with internal teams that want to own the operation long-term

Both modes preserve the same guarantees: GPLv3 code, CC BY 4.0 documentation, free access for newsrooms and non-profit organisations in developing countries, and AI that augments — not replaces — the editorial workflow. Every story is reviewable, editable and overridable by a human editor before publication.

What is open and what is commercial

  • Open forever — pipeline, frontend, documentation, verification trace, and the demo on Data360.
  • Commercial — implementation on a new catalogue, customisation, hosting, training, dedicated editorial support.

This is the model that sustains civic-tech infrastructure across the open-source ecosystem, and it is the model that Abrimos.info already operates for other tools in its portfolio (see About Abrimos.info at the end of this document).

The Challenge demo is a realistic estimate of unit economics for a World Bank-scale deployment. The numbers below come from the actual reference run run2.log (2026-05-29) and from the live MODEL_PRICING constants in lib/ai-client.js.

Token estimates

Sampled Gemini news calls average ~380 input / ~345 output tokens per indicator. Noticia analysis carries the full omnibus context (~18k in / ~2.5k out); Reportaje synthesises multiple Noticias (~35k in / ~5k out).

Estimated daily cost (paid APIs)

Prices as of May 2026: Gemini Flash-Lite at $0.10 / $0.40 per 1M input/output tokens; Anthropic Claude Opus 4.7 at $15 / $75 per 1M (pipeline default D-017).

Stage Steady day Heavy day Provider
News fetch (Gemini) ~11k tok → ~$0.002 ~99k tok → ~$0.02 Gemini API
Analysis (Noticias + Reportajes) ~230k tok → ~$5–6 ~750k tok → ~$15–18 Anthropic Opus
Daily total (paid path) ~$5–6 ~$15–20 Gemini + Anthropic

A full year of paid-API operation on the current 35-indicator / 5-country scope sits in the ~$2–6k range depending on update density. Scaled to the full Data360 catalogue (12,000 indicators across 200+ countries), dynamic discovery keeps the daily delta below 100 changed indicators in steady state — the economics remain in the low five figures per year, well within institutional budget for a flagship editorial channel.

Zero-cost fallback path and data sovereignty

The stack is designed to fall back to $0 marginal cost for clients with budget constraints or strict data-sovereignty requirements:

Provider Env Role Cost
LAIA (Qwen 2.5 14B via vLLM) AI_PROVIDER=vllm Chat default and atomic fallback $0
NVIDIA NIM (Kimi K2.6) AI_PROVIDER=nvidia Analysis (used in run2.log) $0 on Abrimos credits
Claude Opus (Agent SDK) AI_PROVIDER=claude-code Highest-quality narratives (D-017) Subscription / API
Gemini Flash-Lite fetch:news default Headline discovery only ~$0.02/day full pass

Operational rule: run Gemini + NVIDIA/LAIA for daily replay; reserve Anthropic Opus for final narrative polish or premium tiers where Q1 claim traceability is critical. Self-hosting open-weight models (Llama, Mistral, Qwen) is supported out-of-the-box — relevant for public-sector buyers under European, Brazilian or Singapore AI compliance regimes, and for Global South newsrooms that cannot rely on commercial API budgets.

Roadmap milestones

  1. SMTP newsletter — wire subscribers.tsv to a mail provider with the unsubscribe flow already drafted in /privacidad.
  2. NiFi + OpenSearch — replace manual npm replay with scheduled fetch and indexed observations (see architecture-overview.md).
  3. Strategy 2 — headline-data divergence as a third detection signal: when headlines and data disagree, that is the story.
  4. Self-hosted LLM option — packaged deployment for newsrooms and institutions with strict data-residency requirements.
  5. Cross-domain extension (active, externally funded) — see next section.

Continued development beyond Data360: the IPI-funded procurement extension

The same architecture is currently being extended to a second domain — Guatemalan public procurement — under a project funded by the International Press Institute (IPI), in co-design with Ojoconmipisto, a Guatemala City public-interest newsroom. Discovery phase runs April–June 2026; production phase begins July 2026.

This matters for sustainability for three reasons:

  1. Domain validation in production. The pipeline was designed against Data360 indicators but is not Data360-specific. The IPI extension targets a fundamentally different data shape (Guatecompras procurement records), the same agentic and verification architecture. Generalisation is being proven in production, not on a slide.
  2. Independent funding already secured for adjacent work. The roadmap does not depend on the Challenge result. The IPI grant funds the procurement-domain newsletter and the underlying tooling improvements feed back into the Data360 stack — including verification trace generalisation and improvements to the AI-readiness of authoritative public sources.
  3. Newsroom co-design pattern. The IPI work is co-designed with the editor team that will operate it, exactly the same operating contract we propose in §1 to data-publishing institutions. The B2B-with-newsroom-co-design pattern has a working precedent inside Abrimos.info.

Infrastructure

Single Node process behind nginx TLS; optional MCP process on localhost for chat enrichment. No managed cloud dependency beyond LLM API keys. Migratable to fully self-hosted models without code changes.

Upstream contribution to World Bank MCP

Features developed during the Challenge — robust REST fallback, freshness probing, omnibus context for narratives — are candidates for contribution to worldbank/data360-mcp. Abrimos maintains a parallel MCP deployment documented in the README; improvements that benefit all MCP consumers will be proposed as pull requests or design notes to the AI for Data team. Strengthening the upstream MCP directly serves Data360's discoverability and AI-readiness goals.

4. Commercial model and editorial independence

Data360 News Agent joins the Abrimos.info consulting catalogue as a defined offering. The commercial model is not improvised — it sits inside a team that has been delivering custom data infrastructure, evidence-based investigation and training for more than a decade, with both public institutions and public-interest newsrooms among its clients (see About Abrimos.info).

Three primary revenue lines

Line What it covers Who pays
Institutional implementation contracts Operated or Enabled deployment (§1) on a new indicator catalogue: customisation, hosting, training, dedicated support Data publishers: multilaterals, IMF/WB-style entities, regional commissions, national statistical offices, open-data foundations, sub-national governments
Bilateral and foundation grants Adjacent development on shared infrastructure; cross-domain extensions (e.g. procurement, beneficial ownership) — the IPI extension in §3 is the active precedent Public-interest journalism funders, civic-tech foundations, bilateral cooperation agencies
Premium editorial services for large newsrooms Custom indicator watchlists, country-specific newsletter feeds, priority support — strictly opt-in, never a paywall on the public demo Tier-1 LAC and Global South newsrooms with budget for tooling

A recurring fourth source is the Abrimos.info core budget, which covers maintenance of the open staging instance, weekly pipeline replays, and bug-fix releases. None of these revenue lines is required to keep the free tier operational for newsrooms and non-profits in developing countries — the unit economics in §2 make the marginal cost of that free tier negligible against any single paid contract.

Editorial independence as a design property

The product is open source and the verification trace is mechanical: a reader, an editor, or an auditor can reproduce any published claim from Data360 alone, without going through Abrimos or through the institution that funded the deployment. This is the structural answer to the editorial-independence concern that public-interest media funders rightly raise:

  • The funder of an implementation contract has no editorial pre-clearance over what receiving newsrooms publish.
  • The receiving newsroom has full override authority on every generated story before publication.
  • The verification trace (PCN — Proof-Carrying Numbers, the World Bank's open library that we integrate natively) makes the data lineage independently auditable, so accountability does not depend on trust in either Abrimos or the publisher.

Funders fund the pipeline; the newsroom owns the story.

About Abrimos.info

Abrimos.info is a distributed civic-tech organisation founded in Mexico, working from and for Latin America for more than a decade. We build digital infrastructure with information that is fundamental for the region's democracies — combining data engineering, narrative work and training. Our consulting catalogue covers data management environments, custom development, evidence-based investigation, training, and secure reporting tools. The Data360 News Agent extends that catalogue into AI-assisted editorial automation for institutional data publishers.

Three of our projects are directly relevant to this Challenge:

  • TeseoETL — open-source ingestion engine. The Data360 connector is built as an extension of TeseoETL, which has been in production handling government data sources since 2019. Battle-tested foundation, not a new experiment.
  • Sociedad.info — public-procurement and beneficial-ownership explorer for Mexico. Operates today at the scale we project for Data360 News Agent, with the same open-data accountability mission and the same self-hosted, sovereign infrastructure model.
  • Puebla Open Government Diagnostic — for the Coordinación de Municipio Abierto of the Puebla City Council, we used AI to analyse more than 8,000 access-to-information requests and characterise how the right to information is exercised in one of Mexico's most-petitioned municipalities. Findings included improvements in accessibility, categorisation and response time over recent years, and the observation that the most-asked topics are already published under the city's transparency obligations. This is the closest existing precedent to the B2B model proposed in §1: a public institution contracting Abrimos to apply AI to its own data and produce institutionally usable findings.
