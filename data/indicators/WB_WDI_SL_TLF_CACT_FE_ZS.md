## Definition

The labor force participation rate is the labor force as a percent of the population ages 15 and older. The labor force is the sum of all persons of working age who are employed and those who are unemployed.

## Methodology

The labor force participation rate (LFPR) is calculated as follows: 
LFPR (%) = 100 x Labor force / population of a given age group, where the labor force is equal to employment plus unemployment.
Labor force surveys are typically the preferred source of information for determining the labor force participation rate. Population censuses are another major source of data on the labor force and its components.
