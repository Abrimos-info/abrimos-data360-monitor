## Definition

Number of economies that a given economy has direct international postal connections for business-to-business (B2B) shipments with. B2B postal shipments are parcels (starting from 2 kilograms) and express shipments.

## Methodology

The indicator is calculated using dataset supplied by the Universal Postal Union (UPU). It is sourced from UPU's PREDES messaging standards that contains electronic advance information initiated by the origin economy office of exchange (OE) destined for destination economy office of exchange. PREDES protocol is a widely accepted and used standard that is a recommended dataset for volume related network analysis and for the quality-of-service assessments conducted by the UPU. 
Postal items are usually classified into three categories: letter-post items (documents and small parcels up to 2 kilograms), parcel-post items (larger parcels of at least 2 kilograms), and express mail service (EMS). Records associated with parcel-post and EMS mail classes were categorized as business-to-business (B2B) postal activities. 
B2B postal connectivity is the average of the two numbers: the number of unique origin partner economies from which B2B postal items are received throughout a year, and the number of unique destination partner economies to which B2B postal items are shipped during the same time period. 
More details on the methodology can be found here: https://documents.worldbank.org/en/publication/documents-reports/documentdetail/099042226142027181
